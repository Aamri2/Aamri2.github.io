Review

1) Radicals

A radical is a number expressed as the root of another, such as 25 or 343+534-4.

Radicals can be simplified by factoring out perfect squares, such as 50=25×2=52×2=52 or evaluated such as 3144=3122=3×12=36.

They can be multiplied or divided by multiplying the coefficients and the radicands, such as 42×35=4×32×5=1210.

If they have the same radicand or can be simplified to have the same radicand, they can be added or subtracted by adding or subtracting the coefficients, such as 37+57=3+57=87 or 412-33=422×3-33=4×23-33=83-33=53.

To rationalize the denominator means to factor out all radicals in the denominator of a fraction.

2) Line segments

The formula to find the midpoint of a line segment running from point x1, y1 to x2, y2 is x1+x22, y1+y22.

The formula to find the length of a line segment running from point x1, y1 to x2, y2 is x2-x12+y2-y12.

3) Equation of a circle

A circle can be represented by the equation (x-h)2+(y-k)2=r2, where (h, k) represents the centre of the circle and r is the radius of the circle.

4) Properties of triangles

A median is a line segment running from a triangle's vertex to the midpoint of the opposite edge. The intersection of a triangle's three medians is called the centroid, and is the centre of balance of a triangle.

A perpendicular bisector is a line running through the midpoint of an edge of a triangle and perpendicular to the edge. A triangle's circumcentre is the intersection of a triangle's perpendicular bisectors, and represents the centre of the circle that runs through all three of the triangle's vertices.

An altitude is a line segment running through a triangle's vertex and perpendicular to the opposite edge. The intersection of the three altitudes is an orthocentre.

An angle bisector is a line running through a triangle's vertex at an equal angle from both adjacent edges. The three angle bisectors of a triangle intersect at the incentre, which is the centre of the circle inscribed within a triangle.

5) Properties of quadrilaterals

Connecting the midpoints of adjacent sides of a quadrilateral will produce a parallelogram. If the quadrilateral is a parallelogram, it will produce a rhombus.

The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other, and intersect at the centre of the parallelogram. If it is a rhombus, they intersect at a right angle.

Connecting the midpoints of the unparallel sides of a trapezoid will produce a line segment with a length equal to the mean of the lengths of the two parallel sides.

6) Properties of circles

The diameter of a circle runs through its centre.

A chord is the line segment connecting two points on a circle. Its right bisector, the line running through its midpoint perpendicular to the chord, runs through the centre of the circle.

For any three points, only one circle can run through all of them.
