Question 1 [5 points]

Step one: Finding points [2 points]

This question involves finding the side lengths of a quadrilateral formed by the midpoints of the edges of another quadrilateral. As we know, any quadrilateral formed this way will be a parallelogram. Since the opposite sides of a parallelogram are congruent, we know that MN≅OP and NO≅PM, meaning we only need to find the length of one of each pair. For this solution, we will use MN and NO.

MN runs from point M, the midpoint of AB. A is located at (-3, -4), and B is at (21, -4). The formula for the midpoint of a line segment is x1+x22, y1+y22, and if we plug in our values for AB, we get the expression -3+212, -4-42. After we evaluate the numerators, we get the expression 182, -82, and after evaluating that we find M(9, -4).

It ends at point N, the midpoint of points B(21, -4) and C(21, 4), can be found by plugging these points into the same formula, giving us the expression 21+212, -4+42, which evaluates to 422, 02 and subsequently N(21, 0).

NO runs from point N, which we already know the location of, to O, the midpoint of C(21, 4) and D(-3, 6). By plugging these coordinates into our midpoint formula, we get the expression 21-32, 4+62, evaluating to 182, 102 and then O(9, 5).

∴ The points we need to solve this problem are M(9, -4), N(21, 0), and O(9, 5).

Step 2: Finding the lengths [2 points]

The formula to find the length of the line is x2-x12+y2-y12. If we plug in the points for MN, we get the expression 21-92+0-42. By evaluating the parentheses, we get 122+-42. Evaluating the squares gets us 144+16 and then 160. By remembering our rules for simplifying radicals by factoring out perfect squares, we can rewrite this as 16×10, which is equal to 42×10. We can factor out the 42 to give us 410.

By plugging in NO to our length formula, we get the expression 9-212+5-02, evaluating to -122+52 and then 144+25, and finally 169. Since 169 is a perfect square, we can simply rewrite this as 13.

∴ The length of MN is 410 units, and the length of NO is 13 units.

Step 3: Multiplying the lengths [1 point]

Now that we have both of our necessary lengths, multiplying them is the final step. As we know, to multiply two radical expressions, you multiply the coefficient to get the product's coefficient, and the radicands to get the product's radicand. The coefficients here are 4 and 13, which multiply to 52, meaning that is our quotient. Since there is only one radicand involved, 10, it remains unchanged, making our final product 5210.

∴ The product of the lengths of MN and NO expressed as a simplified radical is 5210, meaning X and Y in the puzzle URL, https://aamri2.github.io/XYZ, are 52 and 10 respectively.

Since we have two points of the triangle, X(-12, 16) and Y(-2, 16), as well as the centroid, C(-513, 8), we can calculate the equation of the line running through the midpoint of XY and C, because that would also run through Z. We'll let this point be represented by the letter M.

We know the line running through both M and C will also run through Z. To find the equation of this line, we will use our prior knowledge from grade 9 to find the slope, m, and the y-intercept, b, to give us an equation in the form y=mx+b. Our slope formula is m=y2-y1x2-x1. By plugging in our points M and C, we get the equation m=8-16-513+7, which can be evaluated to m=-853, which can be rewritten as m=-8×35, evaluating to m=-245. If we plug this into our line equation with the values for M, we get the equation 16=-245×-7+b. If we isolate b, we get the equation -b=-245×-7-16, which evaluates to -b=1685-16, which finally evaluates to b=-885. By inserting that into our equation, we get y=-245x-885 as the equation of line MZ.

Now that we have one line running through Z, we simply need one more. We can't get one directly, but we do know that the line YC will run through the midpoint of XZ. By plugging this into our equation for the line, we get the equation y=125x+1045. This equation intersects with the midpoint of XZ. If we plug the known values of this line into the formula for the midpoint, we get x-122, y+162, where x and y are the coordinates of Z, and if we plug these points into our line that passes through the midpoint, we get the equation y+162=125×x-122+1045. This line runs through Z.
